My main research interests are in commutative algebra and its computational aspects. In my thesis I addressed the problem of approximating local rings by Gorenstein rings in the zero-dimensional case, under the supervision of Joan Elias. In particular, I studied how far is a local k-algebra from being Gorenstein and which are its minimal Gorenstein covers, that is, those Gorenstein rings that are at minimal distance. We mainly approach this problem from the perspective of Macaulay's inverse systems. With the help of Bernard Mourrain, I implemented several algorithms in Singular to compute inverse systems and other methods related with the computation of Gorenstein covers.

You can find my thesis available here: Gorenstein colength of Artin local k-algebras.
